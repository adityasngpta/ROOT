While the damage appears concentrated on the leaves in this image, Japanese beetles can also damage the corn's silks, which can affect pollination and kernel development, leading to reduced yield.  

To manage this infestation, a combination of methods is recommended. Firstly, hand-picking the beetles off the plants can be effective for small infestations. Insecticidal soap or neem oil spray can be used to control the beetles, but make sure to apply these solutions in the early morning or evening when temperatures are cooler. Additionally, attracting natural predators like birds and beneficial insects can help control the beetle population. Lastly, preventative measures like planting resistant corn varieties and maintaining a healthy lawn can help reduce the risk of future infestations.
