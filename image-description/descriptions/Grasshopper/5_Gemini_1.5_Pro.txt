Continued feeding by Japanese Beetles can lead to reduced photosynthesis, stunted growth, and ultimately yield loss if left unmanaged.  To control the infestation and protect the crop, I recommend using a combination of strategies.  This includes introducing beneficial insects like ladybugs or lacewings that prey on Japanese Beetle larvae. Additionally, applying a registered insecticide containing carbaryl, permethrin, or bifenthrin can help reduce adult beetle populations. To prevent future infestations, consider planting soybeans earlier in the season to avoid peak beetle emergence times and rotating crops to disrupt their lifecycle. Regularly monitor your fields for signs of Japanese Beetles, particularly during the summer months when they are most active, to ensure timely intervention and minimize potential damage to your soybean yield.
