To effectively manage this infestation, a multi-pronged approach is recommended. This includes using a combination of insecticide applications, such as imidacloprid or clothianidin, to target both adult beetles and larvae. Implementing crop rotation with non-corn crops can break the life cycle of the rootworm, preventing the buildup of populations in subsequent years. Using resistant corn hybrids can also play a role in reducing the impact of rootworm infestations. Additionally, monitoring the field regularly for signs of infestation and adjusting control measures accordingly is crucial to prevent further damage to the crop.
