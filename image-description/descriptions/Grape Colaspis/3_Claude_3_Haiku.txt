Japanese beetles feed on the leaves, flowers, and fruits of plants, skeletonizing the leaves and causing significant defoliation. This can weaken the plant, reduce its ability to photosynthesize, and ultimately lead to decreased yields or even plant death. Additionally, the beetles can spread diseases and create entry points for other pests and pathogens.
