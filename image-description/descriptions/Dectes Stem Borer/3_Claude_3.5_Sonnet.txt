Dectes Stem Borers are particularly problematic because their larvae bore into the stems of plants, weakening them and potentially causing lodging (plants falling over). This can lead to substantial yield losses, especially in soybeans. While the adult beetles feed on leaf tissue and pollen, the most severe damage is caused by the larval stage inside the plant stems.

To manage this pest, a multi-faceted approach is recommended. First, monitoring fields regularly for adult beetles is crucial for early detection. If Dectes Stem Borers are found, consider applying an appropriate insecticide targeting the adult beetles before they lay eggs. However, timing is critical as the larvae are protected once they're inside the stem. Cultural practices such as crop rotation can be effective, as these pests tend to be more problematic in continuous soybean fields. After harvest, destroying crop residue by tillage or shredding can help reduce overwintering populations. Additionally, planting early-maturing varieties and harvesting as soon as crops are ready can minimize losses from lodging. It's also advisable to consult with local agricultural extension services for region-specific management strategies, as the effectiveness of control measures can vary based on local conditions and pest populations.
