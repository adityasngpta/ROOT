The adult beetles, like the one pictured, feed on leaf petioles and stems, but the most severe damage occurs when their larvae tunnel into the main stem of the plant, potentially leading to lodging and yield loss.

To manage this pest, an integrated pest management (IPM) approach is recommended. First, monitor fields regularly for the presence of adult beetles, typically appearing in late June to early July. Consider using pheromone traps to track population levels. Cultural control methods such as early planting and choosing early-maturing soybean varieties can help reduce damage, as plants may be past their vulnerable stages when pest populations peak. Crop rotation with non-host plants like corn or wheat can disrupt the pest's life cycle. If chemical control becomes necessary, foliar insecticides targeting adults may be applied, but timing is crucial for effectiveness. It's important to consult with local agricultural extension services for specific product recommendations and application timing, as these can vary by region. Additionally, harvesting infested fields as early as possible can minimize losses from lodging. Post-harvest destruction of crop residues can also help reduce overwintering populations. By implementing these strategies, farmers can effectively manage Dectes Stem Borer infestations and protect their soybean yields.
