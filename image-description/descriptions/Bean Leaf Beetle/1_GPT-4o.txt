Common signs of Southern Corn Rootworm infestations include ragged holes in leaves, wilting, and in severe cases, root damage which can lead to stunted growth and reduced yields. To manage and control the infestation of Southern Corn Rootworm, consider implementing crop rotation with non-host plants such as soybeans, which can reduce the beetle population. Using appropriate insecticides can also help manage their numbers; consider products containing active ingredients like pyrethroids or neonicotinoids. Moreover, monitoring and identifying early signs of infestation through regular scouting of fields will allow for timely interventions, preventing more severe damage. Implementing integrated pest management (IPM) strategies, including maintaining soil health and encouraging natural predators, can offer long-term control and sustainability.
