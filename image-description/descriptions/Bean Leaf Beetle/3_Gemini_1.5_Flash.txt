If left unchecked, this infestation could lead to significant leaf damage, reducing the plant's ability to photosynthesize and ultimately affecting yield. 

To manage this infestation, several strategies can be employed. **Handpicking** the beetles from the plants, especially during the early stages of infestation, can be effective but may be time-consuming for large fields. **Insecticidal soap** sprays can be used to target the beetles directly. However, it's important to follow label instructions carefully, as repeated applications may impact beneficial insects. **Cultural controls** such as planting trap crops (like buckwheat) nearby can help lure the beetles away from the corn.
